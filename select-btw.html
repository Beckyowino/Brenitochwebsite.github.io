Our Strategic Direction

Brenitoch Primary School is founded on a vision to nurture confident, capable, and compassionate learners through an inclusive and forward-thinking educational approach.

School Targets

  • Provide a safe, inclusive environment where every child can thrive.
  • Foster whole-child development — academic, emotional, social, and physical.
  • Maintain strong teacher–student relationships and high academic standards.
  • Implement evidence-based, high-quality teaching and learning practices.
  • Promote transparent, ongoing communication with families.
  • Ensure excellent pastoral care for student and staff wellbeing.
  • Achieve more than 90% in Attitude, Behaviour, and Effort assessments.
  • Sustain student attendance at or above 92%.

Focus Areas and Strategies

Success for All Students

  • Deliver engaging, differentiated instruction tailored to diverse learners.
  • Use explicit teaching approaches in Literacy and Numeracy.
  • Integrate technology to enhance learning experiences.
  • Encourage positive social behaviour and community responsibility.

High-Quality Teaching and Learning

  • Foster continuous professional development for teachers.
  • Develop comprehensive teaching resources and creative programs.
  • Use data-driven strategies to improve outcomes.

Culture and Care

  • Cultivate respectful, trusting relationships between students and staff.
  • Celebrate diversity and cultural responsiveness.
  • Support student wellbeing and smooth educational transitions.

Community Partnerships

  • Deepen engagement with parents, local communities, and partners.
  • Encourage student involvement in community service.
  • Collaborate with traditional landowners and regional education networks.

Child Rights Protection Policy

We are fully committed to upholding the rights, dignity, and wellbeing of every child entrusted to our care.

Purpose & Scope

This policy safeguards all children at Brenitoch Primary School and ensures a safe, inclusive learning environment in line with the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child and national protection laws.

Core Commitments

  • Zero tolerance for abuse: Any form of abuse or neglect is strictly prohibited.
  • Non-discrimination: Equal protection for every child, regardless of background.
  • Participation: Students’ voices are heard and respected.
  • Safe reporting: Confidential, accessible reporting channels for concerns.
  • Support & recovery: Timely care and counseling for affected children.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Leadership: Appoint a Child Protection Officer and ensure compliance.
  • Teachers & Staff: Undertake regular training and report concerns promptly.
  • Parents & Guardians: Collaborate in promoting safety and awareness.
  • Students: Understand their rights and how to seek help.

Monitoring and Review

The Child Protection Committee reviews this policy annually and collects feedback from students, staff, and parents to ensure continuous improvement.

Data Protection Policy

We value privacy, integrity, and security in managing all personal and school-related information.

Purpose & Scope

This policy ensures that all personal data collected or processed by Brenitoch Primary School is handled lawfully, transparently, and securely in accordance with the Kenya Data Protection Act.

Key Principles

  • Data is collected only for legitimate educational or legal purposes.
  • Only necessary data is stored and retained securely.
  • Accurate and up-to-date records are maintained.
  • Strict access control and encryption protect all sensitive data.

Data Subject Rights

  • Right to be informed about how their data is used.
  • Right to access and correct personal information.
  • Right to request deletion or restriction of certain data.
  • Right to data portability where applicable.

Monitoring & Compliance

The appointed Data Protection Officer (DPO) ensures adherence to data laws, handles subject requests, and oversees incident responses. Regular audits and training sessions promote privacy awareness among staff and parents.
